AI MARIO

a very small neural network learns to run right // genetic algorithm · neat · deep q-learning, no libraries

what is this?

a mario game nobody programmed — a neural network learns to play it.

1. learning method

genetic algorithm — a population of brains; each generation the best are kept, crossed and mutated. parallel and fast, improves in visible jumps, but only learns from the final score.

neat — evolution where the wiring evolves too: brains start minimal and grow neurons & connections, species protect new ideas. slower to take off, but finds small, clever brains.

reinforcement learning (dqn) — one brain learns while playing: each step’s reward nudges the weights by backpropagation. slower and noisier, but learns from every step — ε = how randomly it still acts.

2. ai inputs

what the AI can sense. more inputs = more information, but a bigger network that runs slower and needs more training to make sense of it. a few well-chosen senses often learn fastest.

3. ai outputs

actions it may use. fewer = smaller search space = faster learning. jump+right is a helper combo — enable it to make crossing obstacles much easier.

4. network

the size of the brain. more layers / neurons can express smarter behavior, but train slower and may overfit the training maps. for neat the topology is not yours to set — it grows on its own.

the picture shows the current best brain: green = positive weight, red = negative; node color = activation while watching.

5. training

rewards shape personality: raise coin and it detours for coins; punish jumps and it hops only when needed. all three methods optimize this same score (the dqn receives it per step).

fit last X maps: a fresh random level is added every generation/iteration and scores are averaged over the last X, forcing it to generalize instead of memorizing.

train runs until you stop it · watch best replays the best brain with its senses drawn · new level injects an unseen map.

6. progress

black = best score · grey = average · blue = benchmark: the best brain on a hidden level it never trains on. if black climbs but blue doesn’t, it is memorizing, not learning.
